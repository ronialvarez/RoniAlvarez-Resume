One of the primary objectives of Chicano Studies is the appreciation and preservation of Chicano culture. This field acknowledges the richness of Chicano folklore, music, art, and literature. It sheds focus on the achievements Chicanos have made to American culture and the importance of individuals like Selena Quintanilla, Cesar Chavez, and Frida Kahlo. Chicano Studies promotes cultural preservation in order to stop cultural erasure and ensure that future generations may access their culture. The contribution Chicano Studies makes to the advancement of social justice and fairness is arguably its most notable feature. The field looks at the historical oppression, discrimination, and exploitation that Chicanos faced in order to provide light on systemic inequalities that still exist now. Students who study Chicano Studies get the knowledge necessary to challenge oppressive structures and call for reform. It helps people to struggle for social justice for all oppressed populations, not only Chicanos, by fostering critical consciousness and action.
